Televisions

Televisions are one of the most common appliances in Australian homes â€” and one of the biggest contributors to household energy use. As screen sizes get larger and picture quality improves, energy consumption can rise dramatically if you are not careful.

Screen Size Matters: A 75-inch TV can use more than twice as much energy as a 55-inch model of similar technology. Consider the right size for your room and viewing distance before buying.

Technology Choice: OLED and LED televisions are generally more efficient than older plasma screens. Look for models with high star ratings under the Australian Energy Rating Program.

Usage Habits: Energy consumption also depends on how long you watch TV. Try enabling power-saving modes, lowering brightness, and turning off the TV completely (not just leaving it on standby) when you are not watching.

Our goal is to help you make smarter choices when buying and using televisions, saving you money on electricity bills while reducing greenhouse gas emissions.
