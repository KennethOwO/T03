Data Story: TV Energy Consumption

These visualisations summarise insights from our television energy dataset and show how design choices affect energy use. Question 1 focuses on the Australian market subset, highlighting which screen technologies are most common locally. Questions 2–6 are based on the full dataset, covering trends in screen size, brand variety, power consumption, and efficiency ratings across all entries in the dataset.

1) What type of TV screen technologies are currently available in Australia and which are the most frequent?

We compare counts of models by screen technology. The pie chart shows share, while the bar shows exact counts.

Takeaway: LCD (LED) dominates availability; OLED and standard LCD are much less common.

2) What screen sizes are available, and which are most frequent?

Counts per screen-size category highlight the most common sizes consumers encounter in stores.

Takeaway: Australian shelves are led by 46–65 inch sets, matching current viewing preferences.

3) Which brands have the greatest number of different models?

This ranking shows which manufacturers offer the widest range of models.

Takeaway: The market is competitive, but a few brands dominate the product variety.

4) Which screen technology consumes the least power?

Median average-mode power is compared by technology to avoid distortion from extreme values.

Takeaway: If energy use is your priority, standard LCDs tend to consume less in average mode.

5) What is the relationship between screen size and power use?

A scatter plot of size versus average-mode power shows how demand scales with area.

Takeaway: Bigger screens usually mean higher energy consumption, even within the same technology.

6) What is the relationship between star rating and screen size?

Plotting star ratings against sizes reveals how efficiency labels distribute across the range of sizes.

Takeaway: Star rating and size are not tightly linked; shoppers should check the label for each model.
